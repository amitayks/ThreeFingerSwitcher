## Why

Today every in-overlay navigation is an **odometer**: the recognizer accumulates signed centroid travel and emits a step each time it crosses `stepDistance`, so to move far you physically sweep across the trackpad and hit the edge — which is exactly why an awkward *edge-triggered* auto-repeat exists. There is no notion of "home," so the gesture can't say *hold here and keep going*. The Files navigator already hinted at a better model; we now want one consistent **anchored-joystick** language across the whole platform — launcher grid, Files navigator, and AI canvas — where the spot your fingers land becomes the center, a small offset from it is a direction, and *holding* an offset auto-repeats along a smooth, eased acceleration curve.

## What Changes

- **Anchored local frame.** On the navigation posture (post-activation, fingers relaxed), the recognizer anchors a **center** at the current centroid and a **scale** from the fingers' landing **footprint** (`contacts[].position` spread — already in the data, never read today). Navigation is then read as **offset from that center**, normalized by the footprint, not as accumulated travel. The center re-anchors on every contact-count change (already the re-baseline hook).
- **Zone state machine (per axis).** Crossing the **outer** threshold away from center emits **one** step immediately and arms repeat; returning inside the **inner** threshold **re-arms** (so a quick out-and-back is exactly one step); holding past the outer threshold **auto-repeats**. Left/right and up/down are symmetric; this replaces the odometer interpretation for *navigation* on all surfaces.
- **Eased acceleration replaces edge-repeat. BREAKING (feel).** Auto-repeat is triggered by **dwelling in the offset zone**, not by reaching the physical trackpad edge. The cadence is a **smooth eased curve**: first step immediate, the next after a short initial delay, then the interval shortens along a curve toward a fast floor as the dwell lengthens — never an abrupt slow→fast jump. Applies to **both axes, on every surface**. The old `edgeInterval` hyperbolic ramp and edge-hold triggering are retired for navigation.
- **Opening is unchanged (confirmed).** The four-finger swipe to open the launcher, the three-finger switcher, and the dismiss gestures keep today's odometer-fling activation. The joystick is strictly the *navigate-while-open* model — the same seam the Files drill already sits at.
- **`+1` finger → action menu (generalized).** The Files drill's relative-`+1`-finger morph (`count > baseline`, design D4) becomes a general **action-menu / right-click** intent available across navigation surfaces, not just Files "Open-With."
- **AI canvas resolution moves to two fingers. BREAKING (muscle memory).** The canvas is resolved by a fresh **two-finger** gesture — **down = apply** ("bring it into the document"), **horizontal = dismiss** — instead of today's four-finger swipe. This aligns the grammar: **4 fingers = open/dismiss the platform, 2 fingers = act within it.**
- **New tunables** for the model: footprint→deflection scale, deadzone (inner) / activation (outer) thresholds with hysteresis, initial repeat delay, repeat floor, and the acceleration curve shape — all feel-tuned, live-applied.

### Modified Capabilities
- `gesture-recognition`: replace the odometer interpretation of *post-activation navigation* with the anchored-footprint **positional model** (center + footprint scale + per-axis zone state machine with inner/outer hysteresis); emit a held-in-zone signal (with sign) rather than a held-at-edge signal; generalize the relative-`+1`-finger morph into an **action-menu** intent across surfaces; change the canvas resolution finger count from four to **two** (down = apply, horizontal = dismiss). Opening/activation latches are unchanged.
- `launcher-overlay`: drive the launcher grid **and** the Files navigator from the positional held-in-zone signal; replace the edge-triggered auto-repeat with the **eased, dwell-duration acceleration curve** (immediate first step → short initial delay → curved ramp to a fast floor) on both axes; consume the two-finger canvas resolution.
- `tunable-settings`: add the positional-model tunables (footprint→deflection scale, inner deadzone / outer activation thresholds, initial repeat delay, repeat floor, acceleration curve) — persisted, live-applied.
- `configuration-hub`: surface the positional tunables on the Hub Launcher page grouped as **center / margin / footprint / ease**, with a **live trackpad preview** that draws the deadzone (center), the item & band outer rings (margin), and the user's actual fingertips — scaled to their real footprint — in real time, so the abstract slider values are visible as physical zones around the fingers.

## Impact

- **Recognizer (`Gesture/GestureRecognizer.swift`):** a shared positional interpreter (offset-from-anchor → signed zone, with inner/outer hysteresis and footprint scaling) feeding the existing step / held-state delegate vocabulary; the launcher, Files-drill, and canvas-resolution sub-states route through it. Reads `TouchFrame.contacts[].position` (with a fixed-radius fallback when `contacts` is empty, e.g. test frames). Canvas resolution re-keyed to two fingers.
- **Controller (`Overlay/LauncherOverlayController.swift`):** the `edgeTimer` auto-repeat becomes a **dwell-duration eased curve** keyed to the positional held-in-zone signal instead of `launcherEdgeChanged`/`edgeInterval`; retains the cross-cutting concerns already in `edgeTick` (relayout on band change, files-drill/search resync, dwell reset).
- **Settings (`Settings/AppSettings.swift`):** new positional/repeat tunables (`Defaults` + `Keys` + `didSet` persist), surfaced where the existing step/threshold tunables live.
- **Surfaces:** launcher grid, Files navigator (the in-progress `files-band` drill), and the AI canvas all adopt the one model — the explicit goal of "the same language everywhere."
- **Verification:** the positional interpreter, the zone state machine (out-and-back = 1, dwell = repeat, re-arm on return), footprint scaling + fixed fallback, the eased interval curve (monotonic decay to floor, no abrupt jump), and the two-finger canvas resolution are all Core-testable via `swift build` / `swift test`; existing recognizer tests stay green or are migrated. The MLX/`GemmaRuntime` split is untouched.
- **Docs:** `CLAUDE.md` landmines updated — the canvas resolution is now **two-finger** (revising the documented "fresh four-finger swipe"), navigation is anchored-positional (not odometer), and auto-repeat is dwell-eased (not edge-triggered).
